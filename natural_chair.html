NATURAL CHAIR

Natural Chair was a project I worked on at HackMIT 2016 along we three other students from Arizona and Louisiana. We aimed to build a wheeled robot that could be controlled with brain signals and head movements. Our aim was to eventually implement this technology in wheelchairs to provide a more natural interface for mobility for the disabled.

We built our protype with the MUSE Headband, an Intel Edison powered robot running a NodeJS server, and an Android app interfacing via bluetooth and wi-fi beteen the headband and the Edison respectively.

Our work was featured on the University of Minnesota's College of science and Engineering website. You can find the article here.
