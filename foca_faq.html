Foca FAQ

How Does Foca Use My Data

Foca uses advance computer vision algorithms to determine your focus levels in order to provide you an effective online learning experience. In order to achieve this, we need to be able to access your camera. All of the computer vision runs locally on your machine and none of your camera data ever leaves your computer. Furthermore, Foca only uses your camera while you are watching a video and using the AutoSpeed feature. On most laptops you will be able to know when Foca is using your camera by monitoring the status of the LED next to the camera.

How Does Foca Work

Foca monitors your facial attentiveness using your computer's camera. Our algorithms can detect when you are losing attention. A couple examples include detecting when you are falling asleep or getting distracted by looking, lets say, at your phone. All of our algorithms run locally on your machine, and none of your data ever leaves your computer.

What Websites Does Foca Work On

Foca currently only works on YouTube and sites that embed YouTube videos. We are working to support more popular sites in the coming weeks. If you want us to support a site that is currently unsupported, please let us know by using the feedback form.

How Can I Block Foca on a Site

If you don't want Foca's AutoSpeed button to show up on videos from a certain website, go to settings and add that site's domain name to the "Blacklisted Sites" textbox.
